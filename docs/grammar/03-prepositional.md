# Prepositional Case

!!! abstract "In a nutshell"

    - Prepositional is the "location" case: it answers **где?** ("where — at rest") when used with в/на, and marks the topic after «о» ("about").
    - It is the one case that **never appears without a preposition** — hence the name.
    - Most nouns just add **-е**. Nouns ending in -ия/-ие/-ий take **-ии** instead.
    - A short list of common masculine nouns take a special stressed **-у** ending after в/на (в лесу́, в году́).
    - Some nouns pair only with «в», others only with «на» — this has to be memorized per noun.

## What prepositional is for

Prepositional case shows up in exactly two situations: saying **where** something is located (static, not moving), and saying **what** you're talking/thinking about.

**Кни́га в су́мке.** — lit. *"Book in bag-PREP."* — "The book is in the bag." (location — где?)

**Я ду́маю о тебе́.** — lit. *"I think about you-PREP."* — "I'm thinking about you." (topic — о чём?)

Compare this to [Accusative](02-accusative.md)'s use of the same prepositions в/на for *motion toward* a place — the preposition stays the same, but the case changes depending on whether something is moving or already there:

**Я иду́ в шко́лу.** (accusative — moving *to* school) vs. **Я в шко́ле.** (prepositional — *at* school)

## The basic ending: -е

Most nouns, regardless of gender, simply add **-е** to the stem in the prepositional case.

| Nominative | Prepositional | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| стол → | стол**е́** | table |
| кни́га → | кни́г**е** | book |
| окно́ → | окн**е́** | window |
| маши́на → | маши́н**е** | car |
| теа́тр → | теа́тр**е** | theater |

**Кни́га лежи́т на столе́.** — lit. *"Book lies on table-PREP."* — "The book is lying on the table."

**Я рабо́таю в теа́тре.** — lit. *"I work in theater-PREP."* — "I work in a theater."

Feminine -ь nouns also take -и (not -е) in the prepositional — same pattern you'll see repeat with dative:

| Nominative | Prepositional | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| дверь → | две́р**и** | door |
| ночь → | но́ч**и** | night |

**О две́ри** — "about the door"; **в двери́** — "in the door(way)."

## -ия / -ие / -ий → -ии

Nouns ending in **-ия**, **-ие**, or **-ий** take **-ии** in the prepositional instead of the plain -е. This is a spelling/pronunciation safeguard — it keeps the stem's -и from colliding awkwardly with a bare -е.

| Nominative | Prepositional | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Росси́я → | в Росси́и | Russia |
| ста́нция → | на ста́нции | station |
| зда́ние → | в зда́нии | building |
| san­ато́рий → | в санато́рии | sanatorium |

**Я живу́ в Росси́и.** — lit. *"I live in Russia-PREP."* — "I live in Russia."

**Мы бы́ли на ста́нции.** — lit. *"We were on station-PREP."* — "We were at the station."

## The special stressed -у ending

A short but very common list of masculine nouns take a **stressed -у** (not -е) in the prepositional, but **only** after **в** or **на** in a locational sense — not after «о» ("about").

| Nominative | Normal -е form | Special в/на + -у́ form |
|---|---|---|
| лес (forest) | (о) ле́се | в лесу́ |
| год (year) | (о) го́де | в году́ |
| сад (garden) | (о) са́де | в саду́ |
| шкаф (wardrobe) | (о) шка́фе | в шкафу́ |
| у́гол (corner) | (об) у́гле | в углу́ |

**Я гуля́ю в лесу́.** — lit. *"I walk in forest-PREP(special)."* — "I'm walking in the forest." (location — special -у)

**Я расска́зываю о ле́се.** — lit. *"I tell about forest-PREP."* — "I'm talking about the forest." (topic — normal -е)

Same noun, two different prepositional endings depending on function. This list has to be memorized noun-by-noun; there's no way to predict which masculine nouns take it.

## в vs. на: which preposition goes with which noun?

Both в ("in") and на ("on/at") take the prepositional case for location — but which one goes with a given noun is often arbitrary and has to be learned per noun, much like English "in the car" vs. "on the bus."

| Takes в | Takes на |
|---|---|
| в шко́ле (at school) | на рабо́те (at work) |
| в магази́не (in the store) | на по́чте (at the post office) |
| в теа́тре (at the theater) | на конце́рте (at the concert) |
| в Росси́и (in Russia) | на уро́ке (in class/at the lesson) |
| в кни́ге (in the book) | на у́лице (on/at the street) |

**General tendency (not a hard rule):** в is used for enclosed spaces (buildings, containers, countries); на is used for surfaces, open events, and a set of fixed exceptions (рабо́та, по́чта, ста́нция, уро́к) that just have to be memorized. When in doubt, learn the preposition together with the noun as a single chunk — "на рабо́те" as one unit, not "на" and "рабо́та" separately.

## Prepositional of pronouns

Personal pronouns have their own prepositional forms — always used with a preposition, same rule as nouns.

| Nominative | Prepositional | Example |
|---|---|---|
| я | (обо) мне | Ты ду́маешь обо мне? — "Are you thinking about me?" |
| ты | (о) тебе́ | Я ду́маю о тебе́. — "I'm thinking about you." |
| он / оно́ | (о) нём | Мы говори́м о нём. — "We're talking about him." |
| она́ | (о) ней | Мы говори́м о ней. — "We're talking about her." |
| мы | (о) нас | Он говори́т о нас. — "He's talking about us." |
| вы | (о) вас | Я ду́маю о вас. — "I'm thinking about you (formal/pl.)." |
| они́ | (о) них | Мы говори́м о них. — "We're talking about them." |

Note: «о» becomes **«обо»** before «мне» purely for ease of pronunciation (two consonant-heavy words colliding) — a small, memorizable exception.

**Я ду́маю о тебе́ ка́ждый день.** — lit. *"I think about you-PREP every day."* — "I think about you every day."

## Practice

1. Put «стол» into the prepositional after «на»: **Кни́га лежи́т на ___.**
2. Put «Москва́» into the prepositional after «в»: **Я живу́ в ___.**
3. Put «Росси́я» into the prepositional after «в»: **Мы живём в ___.**
4. Put «рабо́та» into the prepositional with the correct preposition: **Он ___ ___.** (He is at work.)
5. Put «лес» into the prepositional after «в», using the special ending: **Мы гуля́ем в ___.**
6. Put «он» into the prepositional after «о»: **Я не зна́ю о ___ ничего́.**
7. Translate: "The door is in the building (зда́ние)." — mind the -ие ending.
8. Translate: "I'm thinking about you (informal)."
9. Explain why «в саду́» uses -у instead of the expected -е.
10. Fill the correct preposition (в or на) from memory: **Она́ ___ по́чте.** (She's at the post office.)

??? success "Answers"

    1. **Кни́га лежи́т на столе́.** — regular masc. noun, plain -е ending.
    2. **Я живу́ в Москве́.** — regular fem. noun (Москва́ → Москве́), plain -е.
    3. **Мы живём в Росси́и.** — -ия nouns take -ии, not the plain -е.
    4. **Он на рабо́те.** — «рабо́та» is one of the memorized "на" nouns, not "в"; regular -е ending on the noun itself.
    5. **Мы гуля́ем в лесу́.** — «лес» is on the special stressed-у list for в/на locational use.
    6. **Я не зна́ю о нём ничего́.** — «он» → prepositional pronoun «нём» after «о».
    7. **Дверь в зда́нии.** — lit. *"Door in building-PREP."* — "The door is in the building." «Зда́ние» ends in -ие, so it takes -ии.
    8. **Я ду́маю о тебе́.** — lit. *"I think about you-PREP."* — "I'm thinking about you."
    9. «Сад» belongs to the short list of masculine nouns that take a special stressed -у ending after в/на in a locational sense, instead of the ordinary -е — this has to be memorized per noun, it isn't predictable from spelling.
    10. **Она́ на по́чте.** — «по́чта» is one of the fixed "на" exceptions, memorized as a chunk «на по́чте».
