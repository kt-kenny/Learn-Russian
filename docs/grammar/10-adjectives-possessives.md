# Adjectives & Possessives

!!! abstract "In a nutshell"

    - Adjectives agree with the noun they describe in **gender, number, and case** вАФ they change shape constantly, more than the noun itself.
    - There are two adjective "shapes": hard-stem (–љ–ЊћБ–≤—Л–є) and soft-stem (—Б–ЄћБ–љ–Є–є) вАФ the pattern is the same, just –ЊвЖФ–µ and —ЛвЖФ–Є swapped throughout.
    - Spelling rules (–≥ –Ї —Е –ґ —И —Й —З can't be followed by —Л; unstressed –Њ after –ґ —И —Й —З becomes –µ) reshape endings on words like —А—ГћБ—Б—Б–Ї–Є–є, —Е–Њ—А–ЊћБ—И–Є–є, –±–Њ–ї—М—И–ЊћБ–є.
    - –Ь–Њ–є/—В–≤–Њ–є/–љ–∞—И/–≤–∞—И decline like adjectives; –µ–≥–ЊћБ/–µ—С/–Є—Е never change at all вАФ they're frozen genitive pronoun forms, not true possessives.

## Adjectives agree with their noun

An adjective in Russian isn't a fixed word bolted onto a noun вАФ it's a full declining word that mirrors whatever gender, number, and case the noun it describes is in. This means one adjective, like –љ–ЊћБ–≤—Л–є ("new"), has dozens of possible forms depending on context.

### Full paradigm: –љ–ЊћБ–≤—Л–є (hard-stem type)

| Case | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nominative | –љ–ЊћБ–≤—Л–є | –љ–ЊћБ–≤–∞—П | –љ–ЊћБ–≤–Њ–µ | –љ–ЊћБ–≤—Л–µ |
| Accusative (inanimate) | –љ–ЊћБ–≤—Л–є | –љ–ЊћБ–≤—Г—О | –љ–ЊћБ–≤–Њ–µ | –љ–ЊћБ–≤—Л–µ |
| Accusative (animate) | –љ–ЊћБ–≤–Њ–≥–Њ | –љ–ЊћБ–≤—Г—О | –љ–ЊћБ–≤–Њ–µ | –љ–ЊћБ–≤—Л—Е |
| Genitive | –љ–ЊћБ–≤–Њ–≥–Њ | –љ–ЊћБ–≤–Њ–є | –љ–ЊћБ–≤–Њ–≥–Њ | –љ–ЊћБ–≤—Л—Е |
| Dative | –љ–ЊћБ–≤–Њ–Љ—Г | –љ–ЊћБ–≤–Њ–є | –љ–ЊћБ–≤–Њ–Љ—Г | –љ–ЊћБ–≤—Л–Љ |
| Instrumental | –љ–ЊћБ–≤—Л–Љ | –љ–ЊћБ–≤–Њ–є | –љ–ЊћБ–≤—Л–Љ | –љ–ЊћБ–≤—Л–Љ–Є |
| Prepositional | –љ–ЊћБ–≤–Њ–Љ | –љ–ЊћБ–≤–Њ–є | –љ–ЊћБ–≤–Њ–Љ | –љ–ЊћБ–≤—Л—Е |

**–ѓ –≤–ЄћБ–ґ—Г –љ–ЊћБ–≤—Л–є –і–Њ–Љ.** вАФ lit. *"I see new-ACC house-ACC."* вАФ "I see a new house." (masculine inanimate accusative вАФ looks like nominative)

**–ѓ –≤–ЄћБ–ґ—Г –љ–ЊћБ–≤–Њ–≥–Њ —Б—В—Г–і–µћБ–љ—В–∞.** вАФ lit. *"I see new-ACC-animate student-ACC."* вАФ "I see a new student." (masculine animate accusative вАФ looks like genitive)

That animate/inanimate split in the masculine singular and in the plural mirrors exactly what you learned for nouns in the [Accusative Case lesson](02-accusative.md) вАФ animate accusative borrows the genitive form. It's not a separate rule to learn twice, just the same logic applied to the adjective agreeing with its noun.

### Soft-stem type: —Б–ЄћБ–љ–Є–є

A smaller group of adjectives have stems ending in a "soft" consonant (usually –љ preceded by a soft-signaling vowel) and swap every hard vowel in the paradigm for its soft partner: –ЊвЖТ–µ, —ЛвЖТ–Є, —ГвЖТ—О, –∞вЖТ—П.

| Case | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nominative | —Б–ЄћБ–љ–Є–є | —Б–ЄћБ–љ—П—П | —Б–ЄћБ–љ–µ–µ | —Б–ЄћБ–љ–Є–µ |
| Accusative (inanimate) | —Б–ЄћБ–љ–Є–є | —Б–ЄћБ–љ—О—О | —Б–ЄћБ–љ–µ–µ | —Б–ЄћБ–љ–Є–µ |
| Genitive | —Б–ЄћБ–љ–µ–≥–Њ | —Б–ЄћБ–љ–µ–є | —Б–ЄћБ–љ–µ–≥–Њ | —Б–ЄћБ–љ–Є—Е |
| Prepositional | —Б–ЄћБ–љ–µ–Љ | —Б–ЄћБ–љ–µ–є | —Б–ЄћБ–љ–µ–Љ | —Б–ЄћБ–љ–Є—Е |

**–£ –Љ–µ–љ—ПћБ —Б–ЄћБ–љ—П—П —А—ГћБ—З–Ї–∞.** вАФ lit. *"At me blue-fem pen-fem."* вАФ "I have a blue pen."

Compare **–љ–ЊћБ–≤–∞—П** (hard, –Њ) to **—Б–ЄћБ–љ—П—П** (soft, —П) вАФ same job, same position in the word, just the hard/soft vowel swapped. Once you can see that mapping, the soft paradigm stops being "new" and becomes "the same table with different letters."

## Spelling-rule stems: —А—ГћБ—Б—Б–Ї–Є–є, —Е–Њ—А–ЊћБ—И–Є–є, –±–Њ–ї—М—И–ЊћБ–є

Recall the [Alphabet lesson](../alphabet.md)'s spelling rules: –≥ –Ї —Е –ґ —И —Й —З are never followed by —Л (use –Є instead), and –ґ —И —Й —З are never followed by an *unstressed* –Њ (use –µ instead вАФ but a *stressed* –Њ after these letters is fine and stays –Њ). These rules reshape some very common adjectives.

**—А—ГћБ—Б—Б–Ї–Є–є** (Russian) has a stem ending in —Б**–Ї** вАФ since –Ї can't be followed by —Л, the masculine nominative ending is -–Є–є (—А—ГћБ—Б—Б–Ї–Є–є), not "-—Л–є."

**—Е–Њ—А–ЊћБ—И–Є–є** (good) has a stem ending in —И. The stress is on the stem (—Е–Њ—А–ЊћБ—И-), so the ending after —И is unstressed вАФ that means –Њ is not allowed, so it surfaces as -–Є–є: —Е–Њ—А–ЊћБ—И–Є–є, not "—Е–Њ—А–Њ—И–Њ–є."

**–±–Њ–ї—М—И–ЊћБ–є** (big) also has a stem ending in —И. But here the stress falls *on the ending itself* вАФ –±–Њ–ї—М—И–ЊћБ–є вАФ so the "no unstressed –Њ" rule doesn't apply; a stressed –Њ after —И is fine, giving -–ЊћБ–є.

| | —А—ГћБ—Б—Б–Ї–Є–є | —Е–Њ—А–ЊћБ—И–Є–є | –±–Њ–ї—М—И–ЊћБ–є |
|---|---|---|---|
| Masc. nom. | —А—ГћБ—Б—Б–Ї–Є–є | —Е–Њ—А–ЊћБ—И–Є–є | –±–Њ–ї—М—И–ЊћБ–є |
| Fem. nom. | —А—ГћБ—Б—Б–Ї–∞—П | —Е–Њ—А–ЊћБ—И–∞—П | –±–Њ–ї—М—И–∞ћБ—П |
| Neut. nom. | —А—ГћБ—Б—Б–Ї–Њ–µ | —Е–Њ—А–ЊћБ—И–µ–µ | –±–Њ–ї—М—И–ЊћБ–µ |
| Plural nom. | —А—ГћБ—Б—Б–Ї–Є–µ | —Е–Њ—А–ЊћБ—И–Є–µ | –±–Њ–ї—М—И–ЄћБ–µ |

**–≠ћБ—В–Њ —Е–Њ—А–ЊћБ—И–∞—П –Є–і–µћБ—П.** вАФ lit. *"This good-fem idea-fem."* вАФ "That's a good idea."

**–£ –љ–µ—С –±–Њ–ї—М—И–ЊћБ–є –і–Њ–Љ.** вАФ lit. *"At her big-masc house."* вАФ "She has a big house."

The takeaway: these aren't irregular adjectives вАФ they follow the same hard-stem paradigm as –љ–ЊћБ–≤—Л–є, but the *spelling rules* automatically swap —ЛвЖТ–Є and (when unstressed) –ЊвЖТ–µ at the point where those letters would otherwise appear.

## Possessive pronouns: –Љ–Њ–є, —В–≤–Њ–є, –љ–∞—И, –≤–∞—И

These behave like adjectives вАФ they decline for gender, number, and case, and their form agrees with the *noun being possessed*, not with the owner's gender.

| Case | Masc. (–Љ–Њ–є) | Fem. (–Љ–Њ—ПћБ) | Neut. (–Љ–Њ—С) | Plural (–Љ–Њ–ЄћБ) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nominative | –Љ–Њ–є | –Љ–Њ—ПћБ | –Љ–Њ—С | –Љ–Њ–ЄћБ |
| Accusative (inan.) | –Љ–Њ–є | –Љ–Њ—ОћБ | –Љ–Њ—С | –Љ–Њ–ЄћБ |
| Genitive | –Љ–Њ–µ–≥–ЊћБ | –Љ–Њ–µћБ–є | –Љ–Њ–µ–≥–ЊћБ | –Љ–Њ–ЄћБ—Е |
| Dative | –Љ–Њ–µ–Љ—ГћБ | –Љ–Њ–µћБ–є | –Љ–Њ–µ–Љ—ГћБ | –Љ–Њ–ЄћБ–Љ |
| Prepositional | –Љ–Њ—С–Љ | –Љ–Њ–µћБ–є | –Љ–Њ—С–Љ | –Љ–Њ–ЄћБ—Е |

**—В–≤–Њ–є** (your, informal) follows exactly the same pattern: —В–≤–Њ–є / —В–≤–Њ—ПћБ / —В–≤–Њ—С / —В–≤–Њ–ЄћБ.

| Case | Masc. (–љ–∞—И) | Fem. (–љ–∞ћБ—И–∞) | Neut. (–љ–∞ћБ—И–µ) | Plural (–љ–∞ћБ—И–Є) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nominative | –љ–∞—И | –љ–∞ћБ—И–∞ | –љ–∞ћБ—И–µ | –љ–∞ћБ—И–Є |
| Accusative (inan.) | –љ–∞—И | –љ–∞ћБ—И—Г | –љ–∞ћБ—И–µ | –љ–∞ћБ—И–Є |
| Genitive | –љ–∞ћБ—И–µ–≥–Њ | –љ–∞ћБ—И–µ–є | –љ–∞ћБ—И–µ–≥–Њ | –љ–∞ћБ—И–Є—Е |
| Prepositional | –љ–∞ћБ—И–µ–Љ | –љ–∞ћБ—И–µ–є | –љ–∞ћБ—И–µ–Љ | –љ–∞ћБ—И–Є—Е |

**–≤–∞—И** (your, formal/plural) follows the same pattern: –≤–∞—И / –≤–∞ћБ—И–∞ / –≤–∞ћБ—И–µ / –≤–∞ћБ—И–Є.

**–≠ћБ—В–Њ –Љ–Њ—ПћБ –Ї–љ–ЄћБ–≥–∞.** вАФ lit. *"This my-fem book-fem."* вАФ "This is my book." (–Љ–Њ—ПћБ agrees with –Ї–љ–ЄћБ–≥–∞, feminine вАФ not with the speaker's own gender)

**–Ь—Л –≥–Њ–≤–Њ—А–ЄћБ–Љ —Б –љ–∞ћБ—И–Є–Љ —Г—З–ЄћБ—В–µ–ї–µ–Љ.** вАФ lit. *"We speak with our-INSTR teacher-INSTR."* вАФ "We're speaking with our teacher."

### –µ–≥–ЊћБ, –µ—С, –Є—Е вАФ frozen, never decline

Unlike –Љ–Њ–є/—В–≤–Њ–є/–љ–∞—И/–≤–∞—И, the third-person possessives **–µ–≥–ЊћБ** ("his/its"), **–µ—С** ("her/its"), and **–Є—Е** ("their") **never change form**, no matter what noun they attach to or what case that noun is in. That's because they aren't really possessive adjectives at all вАФ they're the frozen genitive case of the pronouns –Њ–љ/–Њ–љ–ЊћБ (–µ–≥–ЊћБ), –Њ–љ–∞ћБ (–µ—С), and –Њ–љ–ЄћБ (–Є—Е), reused to mean "belonging to him/her/them." You'll see this explained again from the pronoun side in [Lesson 11: Pronouns](11-pronouns.md).

**–≠ћБ—В–Њ –µ–≥–ЊћБ –Ї–љ–ЄћБ–≥–∞.** вАФ lit. *"This his book-fem."* вАФ "This is his book." (–µ–≥–ЊћБ stays –µ–≥–ЊћБ even though –Ї–љ–ЄћБ–≥–∞ is feminine)

**–ѓ —З–Є—В–∞ћБ—О –µ–≥–ЊћБ –Ї–љ–ЄћБ–≥—Г.** вАФ lit. *"I read his book-ACC."* вАФ "I'm reading his book." (–Ї–љ–ЄћБ–≥—Г changed to accusative; –µ–≥–ЊћБ did not change at all)

**–Ь—Л –≥–Њ–≤–Њ—А–ЄћБ–Љ –Њ–± –Є—Е –њ–ї–∞ћБ–љ–∞—Е.** вАФ lit. *"We speak about their plans-PREP."* вАФ "We're talking about their plans." (–Є—Е is unchanged; –њ–ї–∞ћБ–љ–∞—Е took the prepositional plural ending)

## –≠ћБ—В–Њ—В / —В–Њ—В вАФ demonstratives that decline

Unlike –µ–≥–ЊћБ/–µ—С/–Є—Е, the demonstratives —НћБ—В–Њ—В ("this") and —В–Њ—В ("that") **do** decline, agreeing with their noun like an adjective:

| Case | Masc. | Fem. | Neut. | Plural |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nominative | —НћБ—В–Њ—В | —НћБ—В–∞ | —НћБ—В–Њ | —НћБ—В–Є |
| Accusative (inan.) | —НћБ—В–Њ—В | —НћБ—В—Г | —НћБ—В–Њ | —НћБ—В–Є |
| Genitive | —НћБ—В–Њ–≥–Њ | —НћБ—В–Њ–є | —НћБ—В–Њ–≥–Њ | —НћБ—В–Є—Е |
| Prepositional | —НћБ—В–Њ–Љ | —НћБ—В–Њ–є | —НћБ—В–Њ–Љ | —НћБ—В–Є—Е |

**—В–Њ—В** follows the same pattern: —В–Њ—В / —В–∞ / —В–Њ / —В–µ.

**–ѓ —Е–Њ—З—ГћБ —НћБ—В—Г –Ї–љ–ЄћБ–≥—Г.** вАФ lit. *"I want this-fem-ACC book-ACC."* вАФ "I want this book."

**–Ю–љ –ґ–Є–≤—С—В –≤ —В–Њ–Љ –і–ЊћБ–Љ–µ.** вАФ lit. *"He lives in that-PREP house-PREP."* вАФ "He lives in that house."

## Short-form adjectives: —А–∞–і, –≥–Њ—В–ЊћБ–≤, –і–ЊћБ–ї–ґ–µ–љ

A small set of adjectives have a **short form**, used only as the predicate ("[subject] is ___"), never in front of a noun. Short forms don't decline by case at all вАФ they only change for gender and number, similar to past-tense verbs.

| | Masculine | Feminine | Neuter | Plural |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| —А–∞–і (glad) | —А–∞–і | —А–∞ћБ–і–∞ | —А–∞ћБ–і–Њ | —А–∞ћБ–і—Л |
| –≥–Њ—В–ЊћБ–≤ (ready) | –≥–Њ—В–ЊћБ–≤ | –≥–Њ—В–ЊћБ–≤–∞ | –≥–Њ—В–ЊћБ–≤–Њ | –≥–Њ—В–ЊћБ–≤—Л |
| –і–ЊћБ–ї–ґ–µ–љ (must/should) | –і–ЊћБ–ї–ґ–µ–љ | –і–Њ–ї–ґ–љ–∞ћБ | –і–Њ–ї–ґ–љ–ЊћБ | –і–Њ–ї–ґ–љ—ЛћБ |

**–ѓ —А–∞–і —В–µ–±—ПћБ –≤–ЄћБ–і–µ—В—М.** вАФ lit. *"I glad-masc you-ACC to-see."* вАФ "I'm glad to see you."

**–Ю–љ–∞ћБ –і–Њ–ї–ґ–љ–∞ћБ —А–∞–±–ЊћБ—В–∞—В—М –Ј–∞ћБ–≤—В—А–∞.** вАФ lit. *"She must-fem to-work tomorrow."* вАФ "She has to work tomorrow."

**–Ь—Л –≥–Њ—В–ЊћБ–≤—Л.** вАФ lit. *"We ready-plural."* вАФ "We're ready."

Notice these short forms never have a noun sitting right next to them the way –љ–ЊћБ–≤—Л–є –і–Њ–Љ does вАФ –Њ–љ–Є always stand alone as the predicate, after the subject.

## Comparatives, briefly

The simplest and most common comparatives are irregular one-word forms you just memorize:

| Adjective | Simple comparative | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| –±–Њ–ї—М—И–ЊћБ–є (big) | –±–ЊћБ–ї—М—И–µ | bigger |
| —Е–Њ—А–ЊћБ—И–Є–є (good) | –ї—ГћБ—З—И–µ | better |
| –њ–ї–Њ—Е–ЊћБ–є (bad) | —Е—ГћБ–ґ–µ | worse |
| –Є–љ—В–µ—А–µћБ—Б–љ—Л–є (interesting) | –Є–љ—В–µ—А–µћБ—Б–љ–µ–µ | more interesting |

**–≠ћБ—В–∞ –Ї–љ–ЄћБ–≥–∞ –Є–љ—В–µ—А–µћБ—Б–љ–µ–µ.** вАФ lit. *"This book more-interesting."* вАФ "This book is more interesting."

**–Ь–Њ–є –±—А–∞—В —Б—В–∞ћБ—А—И–µ –Љ–µ–љ—ПћБ.** вАФ lit. *"My brother older me-GEN."* вАФ "My brother is older than me." (comparatives often take a genitive "than," which you'll recognize from [Lesson 4: Genitive](04-genitive.md))

For adjectives that don't have a short irregular comparative, or in more formal writing, Russian uses an analytic form: **–±–ЊћБ–ї–µ–µ** ("more") placed before the plain adjective.

**–≠ћБ—В–Њ—В –≤–Њ–њ—А–ЊћБ—Б –±–ЊћБ–ї–µ–µ —Б–ї–ЊћБ–ґ–љ—Л–є.** вАФ lit. *"This question more complex."* вАФ "This question is more complex."

At A2 you mostly need to recognize –±–ЊћБ–ї—М—И–µ/–ї—ГћБ—З—И–µ/—Е—ГћБ–ґ–µ/–Є–љ—В–µ—А–µћБ—Б–љ–µ–µ by sound and use them in simple comparisons вАФ full comparative/superlative mechanics go deeper than this lesson covers.

??? success "Answers"

    1. **–љ–ЊћБ–≤–Њ–≥–Њ —Б—В—Г–і–µћБ–љ—В–∞** вАФ animate masculine accusative borrows the genitive form.
    2. **—Б–ЄћБ–љ—П—П** вАФ soft-stem feminine nominative ending.
    3. Because the stem ends in –Ї, and the spelling rule forbids —Л after –≥ –Ї —Е –ґ —И —Й —З вАФ so –Є replaces it, giving -–Є–є.
    4. –±–Њ–ї—М—И–ЊћБ–є has the stress *on* the ending (–ЊћБ–є), where a stressed –Њ after —И is allowed; —Е–Њ—А–ЊћБ—И–Є–є has the stress on the stem, so the unstressed –Њ after —И must become –µ, spelled -–Є–є.
    5. **–Љ–Њ–µћБ–є –Ї–љ–ЄћБ–≥–Є** вАФ feminine genitive singular of –Љ–Њ—ПћБ + genitive of –Ї–љ–ЄћБ–≥–∞.
    6. **–≠ћБ—В–Њ –µ—С –і–Њ–Љ.** вАФ –µ—С never changes regardless of the gender of the noun it possesses.
    7. –µ—С is a frozen genitive pronoun form (not a true adjective), so it never declines to match the case of the noun it modifies вАФ only —Б–µ—Б—В—А—ГћБ takes the accusative ending.
    8. **–≤ —НћБ—В–Њ–Љ –і–ЊћБ–Љ–µ** вАФ masculine prepositional singular of —НћБ—В–Њ—В + prepositional of –і–Њ–Љ.
    9. **–≥–Њ—В–ЊћБ–≤—Л** вАФ plural short form.
