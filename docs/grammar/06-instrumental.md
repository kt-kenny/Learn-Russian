# Instrumental Case

!!! abstract "In a nutshell"

    - Instrumental marks "by means of" — the tool or method used — with **no preposition needed**.
    - «С» + instrumental means "together with" (с молоко́м, с дру́гом) — this «с» is required and is a different job from the no-preposition use.
    - After «быть» ("to be"), professions and temporary states go into instrumental: «он был врачо́м».
    - «Занима́ться» (to be occupied with) and «интересова́ться» (to be interested in) both take instrumental objects.
    - Seasons and times of day fossilize into instrumental adverbs: у́тром, ле́том, но́чью, зимо́й.

## What instrumental is for

Instrumental answers "by what means?" or "with what?" — and unlike every other case pairing you've seen so far, its core use needs **no preposition at all**.

**Я пишу́ ру́чкой.** — lit. *"I write pen-INSTR."* — "I'm writing with a pen."

**Я е́ду авто́бусом.** — lit. *"I travel bus-INSTR."* — "I'm traveling by bus."

There's no word for "with" or "by" in either sentence — the **-ой** ending on «ру́чка» and «авто́бус» carries that meaning entirely on its own.

## Instrumental endings

| Gender | Nominative | Instrumental | Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| Masc. (consonant) | стол → | стол**о́м** | add -ом |
| Masc. (-ь) | слова́рь → | словар**ём** | -ь → -ём |
| Neut. (-о) | окно́ → | окн**о́м** | -о → -ом |
| Neut. (-е) | мо́ре → | мо́р**ем** | -е → -ем |
| Fem. (-а) | кни́га → | кни́г**ой** | -а → -ой |
| Fem. (-я) | неде́ля → | неде́л**ей** | -я → -ей |
| Fem. (-ь) | дверь → | две́рь**ю** | -ь → -ью |

**Он пи́шет карандашо́м.** — lit. *"He writes pencil-INSTR."* — "He's writing with a pencil."

**Она́ ре́жет ножо́м.** — lit. *"She cuts knife-INSTR."* — "She's cutting with a knife." (note ножо́м not ноже́м — after ж/ш/ц, unstressed instrumental -ем becomes -ом when the ending is stressed; here the stress falls on the ending, so -о́м is used — see the spelling note below)

!!! note "Spelling note: -ом vs -ем after hushers"

    After ж, ш, ц, ч, щ, the instrumental ending is spelled **-ем** if unstressed, but **-о́м** if the stress falls on the ending itself. Compare **му́жем** (husband — unstressed, -ем) with **ножо́м** (knife — stressed, -о́м).

## С + instrumental: "together with"

The preposition **с** ("with," in the sense of accompaniment) requires instrumental case — this is a *separate* pattern from the no-preposition "by means of" use above, even though English uses "with" for both.

**Я пью ко́фе с молоко́м.** — lit. *"I drink coffee with milk-INSTR."* — "I drink coffee with milk."

**Я иду́ в кино́ с дру́гом.** — lit. *"I go to cinema with friend-INSTR."* — "I'm going to the movies with a friend."

**Compare the two "withs":**

**Я пишу́ ру́чкой.** (no preposition — the pen is the *instrument* used to write) vs. **Я иду́ с дру́гом.** (с + instrumental — a *companion*, not a tool)

English collapses both ideas into one word, "with." Russian keeps them structurally separate: no preposition for tools/means, «с» + instrumental for accompaniment.

## Professions and states with «быть»

When «быть» ("to be") appears in the past or future tense with a profession, role, or temporary state, the noun describing that role goes into instrumental — not nominative.

**Он был врачо́м.** — lit. *"He was doctor-INSTR."* — "He was a doctor."

**Она́ бу́дет учи́тельницей.** — lit. *"She will-be teacher-INSTR."* — "She will be a teacher."

**Раньше он был студе́нтом.** — lit. *"Before he was student-INSTR."* — "He used to be a student."

In the **present tense**, «быть» is simply omitted (as you've likely noticed from Duolingo), so this instrumental pattern doesn't come up: **Он врач.** — lit. *"He doctor-NOM."* — "He is a doctor." Present tense keeps the profession in nominative; instrumental only appears once a real form of "to be" (was/will be) shows up.

## Занима́ться and интересова́ться + instrumental

Certain verbs simply take their object in instrumental as a fixed rule, without any preposition. The two most useful at this stage:

**Я занима́юсь спо́ртом.** — lit. *"I occupy-myself sport-INSTR."* — "I do sports." / "I'm into sports."

**Я интересу́юсь исто́рией.** — lit. *"I am-interested history-INSTR."* — "I'm interested in history."

There's no deeper logic to memorize here beyond "these verbs govern instrumental" — treat it as a property of the verb, the same way English "interested **in**" vs. "good **at**" pair specific prepositions with specific words.

## Seasons and times of day

A closed set of high-frequency time words are frozen in instrumental form and function as adverbs — you don't decline them further, you just use them as-is.

| Word | Meaning |
|---|---|
| у́тром | in the morning |
| днём | in the afternoon/during the day |
| ве́чером | in the evening |
| но́чью | at night |
| весно́й | in spring |
| ле́том | in summer |
| о́сенью | in fall |
| зимо́й | in winter |

**У́тром я пью ко́фе.** — lit. *"Morning-INSTR I drink coffee."* — "In the morning, I drink coffee."

**Ле́том мы е́здим на мо́ре.** — lit. *"Summer-INSTR we travel to sea."* — "In summer, we go to the sea."

These look like instrumental nouns because historically that's exactly what they are — but at your level, just learn them as fixed adverbs of time, not as a case-ending puzzle to solve each time.

## Instrumental pronouns

| Nominative | Instrumental | Example |
|---|---|---|
| я | мной | Пойдём со мной. — "Let's go with me." |
| ты | тобо́й | Я согла́сен с тобо́й. — "I agree with you." |
| он / оно́ | им | Я говори́л с ним. — "I talked with him." |
| она́ | ей | Я согла́сен с ней. — "I agree with her." |
| мы | на́ми | Он пойдёт с на́ми. — "He'll go with us." |
| вы | ва́ми | Я хочу́ пое́хать с ва́ми. — "I want to go with you." |
| они́ | и́ми | Мы дружи́м с ни́ми. — "We're friends with them." |

Note: «с» becomes **«со»** before «мной» for ease of pronunciation, the same kind of small adjustment you saw with «обо мне» in [Prepositional](03-prepositional.md#prepositional-of-pronouns) — a fixed exception worth just memorizing.

## Practice

1. Put «ру́чка» into instrumental: **Я пишу́ ___.**
2. Put «молоко́» into instrumental after «с»: **Ко́фе ___ ___.**
3. Put «врач» into instrumental for a past-tense profession: **Он был ___.**
4. Fill in the fixed verb pattern: **Я интересу́юсь ___.** (исто́рия)
5. Translate: "In summer, I travel." (е́здить)
6. Put «друг» into instrumental after «с»: **Я иду́ в кино́ ___ ___.**
7. Put «я» into instrumental after «с»: **Пойдём ___ ___.**
8. Translate: "She will be a teacher (fem.)." — учи́тельница
9. Explain the grammatical difference between «Я пишу́ карандашо́м» and «Я иду́ с дру́гом» — why is one instrumental with no preposition and the other «с» + instrumental?
10. Translate: "I do sports." — занима́ться + спорт

??? success "Answers"

    1. **Я пишу́ ру́чкой.** — fem. -а → -ой, no preposition needed for "by means of."
    2. **Ко́фе с молоко́м.** — «с» + instrumental for "together with"; молоко́ → молоко́м.
    3. **Он был врачо́м.** — past-tense «быть» + profession → instrumental.
    4. **Я интересу́юсь исто́рией.** — «интересова́ться» always governs instrumental; исто́рия → исто́рией.
    5. **Ле́том я е́зжу.** — «ле́том» is a fixed instrumental time adverb, no further declining needed.
    6. **Я иду́ в кино́ с дру́гом.** — «с» + instrumental for accompaniment; друг → дру́гом.
    7. **Пойдём со мной.** — «с» becomes «со» before «мной» for pronunciation; я → мной.
    8. **Она́ бу́дет учи́тельницей.** — future-tense «быть» + profession → instrumental; note the -ица → -ицей pattern (regular fem. -я-type instrumental).
    9. «Карандашо́м» is the *tool* used to write, so it takes bare instrumental with no preposition; «дру́гом» is a *companion*, not a tool, so it requires «с» + instrumental — Russian keeps "instrument" and "accompaniment" grammatically distinct even though English uses "with" for both.
    10. **Я занима́юсь спо́ртом.** — «занима́ться» always governs instrumental; спорт → спо́ртом.
