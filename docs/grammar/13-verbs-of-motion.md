# Verbs of Motion (Intro)

!!! abstract "In a nutshell"

    - This is an **introduction only** — the full verbs-of-motion system (14+ pairs, a whole family of prefixes) is a post-A2 topic. Here you get the four verbs you actually need right now.
    - идти́/ходи́ть and е́хать/е́здить each split along **two axes**: one-direction-right-now vs. habitual/round-trip, *and* on-foot vs. by-vehicle.
    - идти́ and е́хать describe a single trip happening now, in one direction; ходи́ть and е́здить describe repeated trips, round trips, or general ability.
    - Prefixes like при- ("arrive") and у- ("leave") attach to these roots to build a much larger system — you'll get exactly a taste of that here, and the rest later.

## Why motion verbs get their own lesson

English uses one verb, "go," for almost everything: "I'm going to the store," "I go to the store every day," "I'm driving to the store." Russian splits this single idea across **multiple verbs**, based on two separate questions:

1. **Is this a single trip happening in one direction right now, or is it habitual/repeated/round-trip/a general ability?**
2. **Are you going on foot, or by some vehicle?**

That's a 2×2 grid, and each cell gets its own verb. At A2 you only need the first, most common pair from each: **идти́/ходи́ть** (foot) and **е́хать/е́здить** (vehicle).

## Idti vs. khodit': on foot

**идти́** describes walking **in one direction, as a single specific trip, happening right now** (or at a specific point in a story).

| | Form |
|---|---|
| я | иду́ |
| ты | идёшь |
| он/она́ | идёт |
| мы | идём |
| вы | идёте |
| они́ | иду́т |

**Я иду́ в магази́н.** — lit. *"I go(one-way,now) into store-ACC."* — "I'm walking to the store (right now)."

**ходи́ть** describes walking as a **habitual, repeated, round-trip, or general-ability** action — not tied to one specific direction or moment.

| | Form |
|---|---|
| я | хожу́ |
| ты | хо́дишь |
| он/она́ | хо́дит |
| мы | хо́дим |
| вы | хо́дите |
| они́ | хо́дят |

**Я хожу́ в магази́н ка́ждый день.** — lit. *"I go(habitual) into store-ACC every day."* — "I go to the store every day."

**Ребёнок уже́ хо́дит.** — lit. *"Child already walks(general-ability)."* — "The child can already walk."

Notice the т→ж mutation in the "я" form of ходить (хожу́) — this is the same kind of 1st-person mutation pattern you saw back in [Lesson 7](07-verbs-present.md).

## Yekhat' vs. yezdit': by vehicle

**е́хать** describes traveling by vehicle (car, bus, train...) **in one direction, as a single specific trip, right now**.

| | Form |
|---|---|
| я | е́ду |
| ты | е́дешь |
| он/она́ | е́дет |
| мы | е́дем |
| вы | е́дете |
| они́ | е́дут |

**Мы е́дем в Москву́.** — lit. *"We go(one-way,now,vehicle) into Moscow-ACC."* — "We're driving/traveling to Moscow (right now)."

**е́здить** describes traveling by vehicle as a **habitual, repeated, round-trip, or general-ability** action.

| | Form |
|---|---|
| я | е́зжу |
| ты | е́здишь |
| он/она́ | е́здит |
| мы | е́здим |
| вы | е́здите |
| они́ | е́здят |

**Я е́зжу на рабо́ту на маши́не.** — lit. *"I go(habitual,vehicle) onto work-ACC on car-PREP."* — "I drive to work (as a routine)."

**Мы ча́сто е́здим в Петербу́рг.** — lit. *"We often go(habitual,vehicle) into Petersburg-ACC."* — "We often go/travel to Petersburg."

## Idti in the past tense — a reminder

You already met this irregular past stem in [Lesson 8](08-verbs-past-future.md), and it's central to talking about motion, so it's worth repeating here:

| Gender/number | Form |
|---|---|
| Masculine | шёл |
| Feminine | шла́ |
| Neuter | шло́ |
| Plural | шли́ |

**Я шёл в шко́лу, когда́ уви́дел дру́га.** — lit. *"I went(masc,one-way) into school-ACC, when saw friend-ACC."* — "I was walking to school when I saw my friend."

е́хать's past tense is regular: **е́хал / е́хала / е́хало / е́хали**.

**Мы е́хали в Москву́ на по́езде.** — lit. *"We went(vehicle,one-way) into Moscow-ACC on train-PREP."* — "We were traveling to Moscow by train."

ходи́ть and е́здить also have regular past tenses: **ходи́л/ходи́ла/ходи́ло/ходи́ли** and **е́здил/е́здила/е́здило/е́здили**.

## Two axes, one decision table

Here's the full picture in one place — remember you're always answering two questions at once: *foot or vehicle?* and *one specific trip right now, or habitual/round-trip/general?*

| | On foot | By vehicle |
|---|---|---|
| **One direction, specific trip, now** | идти́ | е́хать |
| **Habitual / repeated / round-trip / general ability** | ходи́ть | е́здить |

**Он идёт в парк.** — lit. *"He goes(one-way,foot) into park-ACC."* — "He's walking to the park (right now)." — one trip, on foot, in progress

**Он хо́дит в парк ка́ждое у́тро.** — lit. *"He goes(habitual,foot) into park-ACC every morning."* — "He goes to the park every morning." — repeated, on foot

**Она́ е́дет в аэропо́рт.** — lit. *"She goes(one-way,vehicle) into airport-ACC."* — "She's on her way to the airport." — one trip, by vehicle, in progress

**Она́ е́здит в аэропо́рт ка́ждый ме́сяц.** — lit. *"She goes(habitual,vehicle) into airport-ACC every month."* — "She travels to the airport every month." — repeated, by vehicle

If you're ever unsure which verb to reach for, ask yourself these two questions in order: **(1) Am I on foot or in a vehicle?** — picks идти́/ходи́ть vs. е́хать/е́здить. **(2) Am I describing one specific trip happening right now (or at one moment in a story), or something repeated/habitual/round-trip?** — picks идти́/е́хать vs. ходи́ть/е́здить.

## The perfective "set off": пойти́ / пое́хать

идти́ and е́хать are imperfective, describing the ongoing trip itself. Their perfective partners, **пойти́** and **пое́хать**, mean **"to set off / head out"** — the single completed moment of *starting* the trip, not the whole journey.

**Я пошёл в магази́н.** — lit. *"I set-off(masc,perf) into store-ACC."* — "I headed to the store." (the moment of leaving — you're now on your way, or already gone)

**Мы пое́дем в Москву́ за́втра.** — lit. *"We will-set-off(perf-future) into Moscow-ACC tomorrow."* — "We'll head to Moscow tomorrow."

**Он пошёл домо́й.** — lit. *"He set-off(masc,perf) homeward."* — "He left for home / He's gone home."

This is exactly the aspect distinction from [Lesson 9](09-aspect.md) applied to motion verbs: идти́ (imperfective) = the ongoing trip; пойти́ (perfective) = the single bounded act of departing.

## A prefix teaser — and where the rest of this lives

идти́ and е́хать are actually the **roots** of a much bigger family. Small prefixes attach to them to build precise directional verbs — arriving, leaving, entering, crossing, and more. You only need two examples right now, just to see the shape of the system:

**прийти́** (from при- + идти́) means **"to arrive" (on foot)**.

**Он придёт в семь часо́в.** — lit. *"He will-arrive(perf) at seven hour-GEN."* — "He'll arrive at seven o'clock."

**уйти́** (from у- + идти́) means **"to leave / depart" (on foot)**.

**Она́ ушла́ ра́но.** — lit. *"She left(fem,perf) early."* — "She left early."

That's it for now — **the full prefix system (при-, у-, в-, вы-, до-, за-, etc., applied to all four roots you learned above, plus more) is a substantial topic on its own and is deliberately deferred to a later, post-A2 lesson.** For A2, being solid on идти́/ходи́ть, е́хать/е́здить, and their basic perfective пойти́/пое́хать is genuinely enough to describe your daily movements, plans, and simple travel stories.

## Practice

1. Conjugate **идти́** for "мы" and "они́."
2. Conjugate **ходи́ть** for "я" and "ты." What consonant mutation happens?
3. Choose идти́ or ходи́ть: Я ___ в шко́лу сейча́с. (right now, one trip)
4. Choose идти́ or ходи́ть: Я ___ в шко́лу ка́ждый день. (every day, habitual)
5. Choose е́хать or е́здить: Мы ___ в о́тпуск ка́ждое ле́то. (every summer, habitual)
6. Choose е́хать or е́здить: Мы ___ на мо́ре сейча́с. (right now, one trip)
7. Give the masculine and feminine past tense of идти́.
8. Translate using пойти́: "She headed to work." (feminine subject)
9. Translate: "He will arrive tomorrow." (use прийти́)
10. Translate: "She left early." (use уйти́, feminine)
11. True or false: ходи́ть can describe a general ability, like a child learning to walk.
12. In one sentence, explain the two axes that distinguish идти́, ходи́ть, е́хать, and е́здить from each other.

??? success "Answers"

    1. мы **идём**, они́ **иду́т** — regular идти́ present tense.
    2. я **хожу́**, ты **хо́дишь** — т→ж mutation in the "я" form only.
    3. **иду́** — one specific trip, happening now.
    4. **хожу́** — habitual, repeated action.
    5. **е́здим** — habitual, repeated, by vehicle.
    6. **е́дем** — one specific trip, right now, by vehicle.
    7. **шёл** (masculine), **шла́** (feminine) — irregular past stem.
    8. **Она́ пошла́ на рабо́ту.** — perfective пойти́, feminine past, "set off."
    9. **Он придёт за́втра.** — prefixed perfective, при- = "arrive."
    10. **Она́ ушла́ ра́но.** — prefixed perfective, у- = "leave/depart."
    11. **True** — ходи́ть covers general ability/habitual walking, not just repeated round trips.
    12. The two axes are: (1) on foot vs. by vehicle, and (2) a single trip in one direction happening now vs. habitual/repeated/round-trip/general ability.
