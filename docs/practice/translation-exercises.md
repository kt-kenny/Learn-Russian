# Translation Exercises

English to Russian, in three graded sets. Write out a full sentence for each — don't just think the answer, the act of producing the endings yourself is the whole point. Model answers include acceptable alternatives where Russian genuinely allows more than one correct phrasing.

## Set A — Simple sentences (weeks 5–10 level)

Basic vocabulary, single clauses, present tense and simple statements.

1. I have a dog.
2. She lives in Moscow.
3. We are reading a book.
4. Where is the museum?
5. This is my brother.
6. I don't understand you.
7. He works at a school.
8. Do you speak Russian?
9. I like this city.
10. There is no milk in the fridge.

??? success "Answers"

    1. **У меня́ есть соба́ка.** — у + genitive меня́ is Russian's "have" construction; собака stays nominative as the grammatical subject. *(Alternative: drop есть in casual speech — У меня́ соба́ка — acceptable when just stating possession, not confirming existence.)*
    2. **Она́ живёт в Москве́.** — живёт is 3rd person singular of жить; в + prepositional Москве́ for location.
    3. **Мы чита́ем кни́гу.** — чита́ем is мы-form present; кни́гу is accusative direct object (feminine -а → -у).
    4. **Где нахо́дится музе́й?** — нахо́дится is the standard "is located" verb. *(Alternative: simpler Где музе́й? is also completely natural and common.)*
    5. **Э́то мой брат.** — Э́то + nominative for "this is X" identification sentences; no verb needed.
    6. **Я тебя́ не понима́ю.** — тебя́ is accusative of ты, placed before the verb (natural word order); не goes right before понима́ю. *(Alternative: Я не понима́ю тебя́ is also grammatical, slightly more emphatic on тебя́.)*
    7. **Он рабо́тает в шко́ле.** — рабо́тает + в + prepositional шко́ле for workplace.
    8. **Ты говори́шь по-ру́сски?** (informal) / **Вы говори́те по-ру́сски?** (formal) — по-ру́сски is an unchanging adverb, not a noun.
    9. **Мне нра́вится э́тот го́род.** — dative construction: мне нра́вится + nominative subject э́тот го́род (город is what's "pleasing," not the object of a verb).
    10. **В холоди́льнике нет молока́.** — нет + genitive молока́ for negated existence; в холоди́льнике is prepositional for location. *(Alternative: Молока́ нет в холоди́льнике, reordered for emphasis on "milk," is also fine.)*

## Set B — Medium (verbs + cases combined)

Requires coordinating case endings with verb conjugation in the same sentence, plus some past tense.

1. I gave the book to my sister.
2. We went to the store yesterday.
3. He doesn't have any money.
4. She is writing a letter to her friend.
5. I need to buy bread and milk.
6. They live next to the park.
7. I saw my teacher at the station.
8. Can you help me, please?
9. We are satisfied with the result.
10. I am afraid of dogs.

??? success "Answers"

    1. **Я дал(а́) кни́гу сестре́.** — дал/дала́ agrees with speaker's gender; кни́гу is accusative direct object; сестре́ is dative indirect object ("to sister").
    2. **Вчера́ мы пошли́ в магази́н.** — пошли́ is perfective past of пойти́ (a single completed trip); в + accusative магази́н for destination ("into the store").
    3. **У него́ нет де́нег.** — у + genitive него́ for "have," negated with нет + genitive plural де́нег (де́ньги is a plural-only noun).
    4. **Она́ пи́шет письмо́ подру́ге.** — пи́шет present imperfective (ongoing action); письмо́ accusative object; подру́ге dative indirect object.
    5. **Мне ну́жно купи́ть хлеб и молоко́.** — дative мне ну́жно + infinitive купи́ть; хлеб and молоко́ both stay in the accusative-looking form (inanimate, no change from nominative).
    6. **Они́ живу́т ря́дом с па́рком.** — ря́дом с + instrumental па́рком.
    7. **Я ви́дел(а) свою́ учи́тельницу на ста́нции.** — ви́дел/ви́дела past agreeing with speaker; свою́ учи́тельницу accusative (animate feminine — but feminine animate accusative equals the regular -у ending, not genitive, unlike masculine); на ста́нции prepositional.
    8. **Помоги́те мне, пожа́луйста?** (formal) / **Помоги́ мне, пожа́луйста?** (informal) — imperative + dative мне (the person helped is always dative with помо́чь).
    9. **Мы дово́льны результа́том.** — дово́льны (plural short-form adjective) governs instrumental результа́том.
    10. **Я бою́сь соба́к.** — боя́ться governs the genitive, and here соба́к is genitive plural (fear of dogs in general, not one specific dog).

## Set C — Harder (aspect, opinions, time expressions)

Requires choosing between aspect pairs, forming opinions, and handling time expressions correctly.

1. I was reading a book when you called.
2. I finished the book yesterday.
3. I think that this is a good idea.
4. Every morning I drink coffee and read the news.
5. Yesterday we finally found the museum.
6. I will call you tomorrow at five o'clock.
7. It seems to me that he is right.
8. While she was cooking dinner, I set the table.
9. He usually gets up at seven, but today he got up at nine.
10. I have been living in this city for two years.

??? success "Answers"

    1. **Я чита́л(а) кни́гу, когда́ ты позвони́л(а).** — чита́л imperfective for the ongoing background action; позвони́л perfective for the single interrupting event — the classic "process interrupted by event" aspect pattern.
    2. **Вчера́ я дочита́л(а) кни́гу.** — perfective for a single completed result with a clear endpoint ("finished," not just "read for a while"). *(Alternative: прочита́л(а) also works and is more common in general use; дочита́л(а) specifically emphasizes finishing something already started.)*
    3. **Я ду́маю, что э́то хоро́шая иде́я.** — что is required as "that," unlike optional English "that"; хоро́шая agrees with feminine иде́я.
    4. **Ка́ждое у́тро я пью ко́фе и чита́ю но́вости.** — both verbs imperfective, triggered by "ка́ждое у́тро" (habitual repeated action).
    5. **Вчера́ мы наконе́ц нашли́ музе́й.** — нашли́ perfective, one-time completed discovery, reinforced by "наконе́ц" (finally) signaling an achieved result.
    6. **Я позвоню́ тебе́ за́втра в пять часо́в.** — позвоню́ perfective future (single planned call); тебе́ dative (person called); в + accusative пять часо́в for clock time.
    7. **Мне ка́жется, что он прав.** — dative ка́жется construction; прав is the short-form masculine adjective agreeing with он.
    8. **Пока́ она́ гото́вила у́жин, я накры́л(а) на стол.** — гото́вила imperfective for the background process (пока́ = "while"); накры́л(а) perfective for the single completed action of setting the table.
    9. **Обы́чно он встаёт в семь, но сего́дня он встал в де́вять.** — встаёт imperfective habitual; встал perfective for the one specific completed instance today — the same verb pair (вставать/встать) doing both jobs in one sentence.
    10. **Я живу́ в э́том го́роде уже́ два го́да.** — Russian uses the *present* tense with "уже́ + duration" for an action still ongoing (unlike English's "have been living"); в э́том го́роде prepositional.
