# Shopping & Money

Prices, trying things on, and the small talk that goes with a purchase. Numbers and prices lean on [genitive](../grammar/04-genitive.md) — see [Numbers & Time](../grammar/12-numbers-time.md) for the full rules on numbers and cases.

## Asking the price

| Russian | Literal | Means | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ско́лько э́то сто́ит?** | "How-much this costs?" | "How much does this cost?" | сто́ит is 3rd person singular of сто́ить — used regardless of what "this" is. |
| **Ско́лько сто́ят...?** | "How-much cost...?" | "How much are...?" (plural items) | Plural verb сто́ят when asking about several things at once. |
| **Ско́лько с меня́?** | "How-much from me?" | "How much do I owe?" | с + [genitive](../grammar/04-genitive.md); common at a market stall. |
| **Э́то до́рого** | "This expensively" | "That's expensive" | до́рого used predicatively, no noun agreement needed. |
| **Э́то дёшево** | "This cheaply" | "That's cheap" | Same predicative pattern as до́рого. |
| **У вас есть что-нибу́дь деше́вле?** | "At you is anything cheaper?" | "Do you have anything cheaper?" | деше́вле is the comparative of дешёвый — irregular, doesn't add -ее. |
| **Э́то сли́шком до́рого** | "This too expensively" | "That's too expensive" | сли́шком intensifies до́рого. |

!!! tip "Pattern"

    «Ско́лько + [verb] + [subject]?» is your price-asking skeleton: singular thing → сто́ит, plural things → сто́ят. Ско́лько сто́ит биле́т? vs. Ско́лько сто́ят биле́ты? — the verb agrees with what's being priced, not with "how much."

## Paying

| Russian | Literal | Means | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Мо́жно (за)плати́ть ка́ртой?** | "[Is it] possible to-pay by-card?" | "Can I pay by card?" | ка́ртой is [instrumental](../grammar/06-instrumental.md) — the means of payment. |
| **Мо́жно нали́чными?** | "[Is it] possible with-cash?" | "Can I pay cash?" | нали́чными is also instrumental — same pattern as ка́ртой. |
| **У вас есть сда́ча?** | "At you is change?" | "Do you have change?" | Standard у-есть construction — see [genitive](../grammar/04-genitive.md). |
| **Э́то всё** | "This [is] all" | "That's everything" | Said when you're done adding items, before paying. |
| **Чек, пожа́луйста** | "Receipt, please" | "Receipt, please" | чек covers both "receipt" and, in other contexts, "check/cheque." |
| **Мо́жно без че́ка** | "[It's] possible without receipt" | "I don't need a receipt" | без + [genitive](../grammar/04-genitive.md) — same без pattern as без са́хара. |
| **Здесь есть ски́дка?** | "Here is [a] discount?" | "Is there a discount?" | ски́дка ("discount") is feminine. |
| **Э́то со ски́дкой?** | "This with discount?" | "Is this discounted?" | со ски́дкой is instrumental — the "with" pattern again. |

!!! tip "Pattern"

    "Pay by/with X" always uses the bare [instrumental](../grammar/06-instrumental.md) — no preposition needed: плати́ть ка́ртой, плати́ть нали́чными. This is different from English "pay *by* card," which needs "by." Dropping the preposition and just using the instrumental ending is correct Russian, not a shortcut.

## Browsing and trying things on

| Russian | Literal | Means | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Я про́сто смотрю́** | "I simply am-looking" | "Just looking" | Standard, polite way to wave off a salesperson. |
| **Мо́жно поме́рить?** | "[Is it] possible to-try-on?" | "Can I try this on?" | поме́рить (perfective) — a one-time completed action of trying something. |
| **Где приме́рочная?** | "Where [is the] fitting-room?" | "Where's the fitting room?" | приме́рочная is a substantivized adjective (functions as a noun) — feminine by form. |
| **У вас есть друго́й разме́р?** | "At you is [a] different size?" | "Do you have another size?" | друго́й agrees with masculine разме́р. |
| **Каки́е у вас есть разме́ры?** | "What at you are sizes?" | "What sizes do you have?" | Plural version of the у-есть construction. |
| **Мой разме́р — M** | "My size — M" | "My size is M" | Sizes are often given as international letters even in Russian stores. |
| **Э́то мне велико́** | "This to-me [is] too-big" | "This is too big on me" | Dative мне + short-form adjective велико́ — see [dative](../grammar/05-dative.md). |
| **Э́то мне мало́** | "This to-me [is] too-small" | "This is too small on me" | Same dative pattern, opposite meaning. |
| **Э́то мне подхо́дит** | "This to-me fits" | "This fits me" | подхо́дит governs dative мне — literally "approaches/suits to me." |

!!! tip "Pattern"

    Fit-related judgments (too big, too small, fits, suits) route through the [dative](../grammar/05-dative.md): Э́то мне велико́ / мало́ / подхо́дит / идёт. "To me" always comes first, mirroring мне ну́жно and мне нра́вится from other pages — the dative marks the person experiencing something, not the person doing something.

## Deciding and closing the purchase

| Russian | Literal | Means | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Я э́то возьму́** | "I this will-take" | "I'll take it" | возьму́ is perfective future of взять. |
| **Я поду́маю** | "I will-think" | "I'll think about it" | Polite way to decline without saying no — perfective future of поду́мать. |
| **Мне ну́жно подожда́ть** | "To-me [it is] necessary to-wait" | "I need to wait / think it over" | Same мне ну́жно + infinitive pattern as elsewhere. |
| **Заверни́те, пожа́луйста** | "Wrap, please" | "Could you wrap it, please" | Formal imperative of заверну́ть — for gift purchases. |
| **Дайте, пожа́луйста, паке́т** | "Give, please, [a] bag" | "Could I get a bag, please" | Formal imperative of дать; irregular verb worth knowing on sight. |
| **Э́то в пода́рок** | "This as [a] gift" | "It's a gift" | в пода́рок is a fixed accusative expression, doesn't change. |

!!! tip "Pattern"

    Perfective future verbs like возьму́, поду́маю, заплачу́ describe a single decisive action you're about to complete — the natural aspect for "I'll do X" in the moment of deciding. See [aspect](../grammar/09-aspect.md) once you get there; for now, just recognize that these "I will..." forms conjugate like present tense but mean future because the verb is perfective.
