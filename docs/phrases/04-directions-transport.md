# Directions & Transport

Asking where things are, getting there, and surviving public transport. Heavy on [prepositional](../grammar/03-prepositional.md) (location) and [genitive](../grammar/04-genitive.md) (proximity words like near/far).

## Asking where something is

| Russian | Literal | Means | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Где нахо́дится...?** | "Where is-located...?" | "Where is...?" | нахо́дится is reflexive, the "proper" way to ask location; more formal than a bare Где. |
| **Где здесь...?** | "Where here...?" | "Where's the nearest...?" | Casual — implies "around here." |
| **Как дойти́ до...?** | "How to-reach-on-foot to...?" | "How do I get to... (on foot)?" | до + [genitive](../grammar/04-genitive.md); дойти́ specifically implies walking. |
| **Как дое́хать до...?** | "How to-reach-by-vehicle to...?" | "How do I get to... (by transport)?" | Same до + genitive pattern, but дое́хать implies riding something. |
| **Э́то далеко́?** | "This far?" | "Is it far?" | далеко́ is a predicate adverb — no verb needed. |
| **Э́то бли́зко** | "This close" | "It's close" | Common reassuring reply. |
| **Э́то далеко́ отсю́да?** | "This far from-here?" | "Is it far from here?" | отсю́да is a fixed adverb, doesn't decline. |
| **Ско́лько идти́ пешко́м?** | "How-much to-walk on-foot?" | "How long is the walk?" | идти́ пешко́м specifically means "go on foot," redundant-sounding but standard. |

!!! tip "Pattern"

    «Как дойти́/дое́хать до + [genitive]?» is the core "how do I get to X" template — до always takes [genitive](../grammar/04-genitive.md), so музе́й ("museum") becomes до музе́я, and вокза́л ("station") becomes до вокза́ла. Pick дойти́ if you're walking, дое́хать if you're on wheels.

## Near, far, left, right

| Russian | Literal | Means | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ря́дом с...** | "Alongside with..." | "Next to..." | Takes [instrumental](../grammar/06-instrumental.md) — Ря́дом с гости́ницей ("next to the hotel"). |
| **Напро́тив...** | "Opposite [of]..." | "Across from..." | Takes [genitive](../grammar/04-genitive.md) — Напро́тив вокза́ла. |
| **Недалеко́ от...** | "Not-far from..." | "Not far from..." | от + [genitive](../grammar/04-genitive.md). |
| **Нале́во** | "To-the-left" | "[Turn/go] left" | Motion direction — different from нале́ве, which doesn't exist; this is the "where to" form. |
| **Напра́во** | "To-the-right" | "[Turn/go] right" | Same pattern as нале́во. |
| **Пря́мо** | "Straight" | "Straight ahead" | Also means "exactly/directly" in other contexts. |
| **На углу́** | "On [the] corner" | "At the corner" | на + [prepositional](../grammar/03-prepositional.md); у́гол → углу́ shows the fleeting-vowel pattern. |
| **Поверни́те нале́во** | "Turn to-the-left" | "Turn left" | Formal imperative of поверну́ть. |
| **Иди́те пря́мо, пото́м напра́во** | "Go straight, then to-the-right" | "Go straight, then turn right" | Chaining directions — standard structure for multi-step directions. |

!!! tip "Pattern"

    Direction words ending in **-о** (нале́во, напра́во) answer "where to?"; the same idea expressed as a static location uses **на** + [prepositional](../grammar/03-prepositional.md) with сле́ва/спра́ва ("on the left/right") instead — Магази́н сле́ва ("The shop is on the left"). Motion vs. location is a recurring Russian distinction; see [verbs of motion](../grammar/13-verbs-of-motion.md).

## Public transport

| Russian | Literal | Means | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Кака́я марш­ру́тка/авто́бус идёт до...?** | "Which minibus/bus goes to...?" | "Which bus goes to...?" | идёт here means "travels/runs," not literally "walks." |
| **Оди́н биле́т, пожа́луйста** | "One ticket, please" | "One ticket, please" | оди́н agrees with masculine биле́т — see [numbers](../grammar/12-numbers-time.md). |
| **Кака́я э́то остано́вка?** | "Which this stop?" | "What stop is this?" | остано́вка is feminine, hence кака́я not како́й. |
| **Ско́лько остано́вок до...?** | "How-many stops to...?" | "How many stops until...?" | остано́вок is genitive plural after ско́лько. |
| **Мне выходи́ть здесь?** | "To-me to-exit here?" | "Do I get off here?" | Dative + infinitive impersonal construction — see [dative](../grammar/05-dative.md). |
| **Вы выхо́дите?** | "You are-exiting?" | "Are you getting off?" (at the next stop) | Standard thing to ask someone blocking your exit on a crowded bus. |
| **Следующая остано́вка...** | "[The] next stop..." | "Next stop is..." | What you'll hear announced — сле́дующая agrees with feminine остано́вка. |

!!! tip "Pattern"

    «Мне + [infinitive]?» is a compact way to ask "should I / do I need to [verb]?": Мне выходи́ть здесь?, Мне пересе́сть? ("Do I need to transfer?"). The dative мне plus a bare infinitive is a whole category of impersonal Russian questions — see [dative](../grammar/05-dative.md).

## Metro-specific phrases

| Russian | Literal | Means | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Где ближа́йшее метро́?** | "Where nearest metro?" | "Where's the nearest metro?" | метро́ is indeclinable neuter — never changes form. |
| **Кака́я э́то ли́ния?** | "Which this line?" | "Which line is this?" | ли́ния is feminine. |
| **Мне ну́жно на ста́нцию...** | "To-me [it is] necessary to [the] station..." | "I need to get to ... station" | на + accusative for a destination station; see [dative](../grammar/05-dative.md) for the мне ну́жно pattern. |
| **Э́та ли́ния идёт до...?** | "This line goes to...?" | "Does this line go to...?" | idiomatic use of идёт for a transit line "running" somewhere. |
| **Перехо́д на...** | "Transfer to..." | "Transfer to [line/station]" | What's printed on metro signs for connecting lines. |

## Lost, and asking for help finding your way

| Russian | Literal | Means | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Я потеря́лся / потеря́лась** | "I got-lost (m/f)" | "I'm lost" | Reflexive past tense; -лся for men, -лась for women — see [past tense](../grammar/08-verbs-past-future.md). |
| **Я не зна́ю, где я** | "I not know where I" | "I don't know where I am" | No "am" needed — same missing-"to be" pattern as everywhere else. |
| **Вы не подска́жете, где...?** | "You not would-tell, where...?" | "Could you tell me where...?" | Extremely common polite opener — literally "you wouldn't tell me," softened with не. |
| **Покажи́те, пожа́луйста, на ка́рте** | "Show, please, on [the] map" | "Could you show me on the map?" | Formal imperative of показа́ть; на ка́рте uses [prepositional](../grammar/03-prepositional.md). |
| **У вас есть ка́рта?** | "At you is [a] map?" | "Do you have a map?" | у вас есть — standard "do you have" construction; see [genitive](../grammar/04-genitive.md). |
| **Я здесь впервы́е** | "I here for-the-first-time" | "I'm here for the first time" | Useful context to add when asking for help — people tend to explain more slowly. |

!!! tip "Pattern"

    «Вы не подска́жете...?» is the politest possible way to ask a stranger anything — softer than a direct question, roughly "would you happen to know...?" Follow it with где, как, or ско́лько and you can ask about location, method, or quantity with the same opener.
